# Call Transcript: Discovery Call — DataFlow Inc
**Date:** January 15, 2026 | **Duration:** 42 minutes
**Rep:** Jordan Mitchell | **Prospect:** Priya Sharma, VP of Engineering
**Deal:** DataFlow Inc — Enterprise Platform License ($156,000)
**Call Type:** Discovery | **Recording ID:** REC-2026-0115-001

---

**[0:00] Jordan:** Priya, thanks for taking the time today. Before we dive in, I did some homework on DataFlow — I saw you just closed your Series B and are scaling the sales team. Congratulations.

**[0:15] Priya:** Thanks, Jordan. Yes, it's been a busy few months. That's actually why we're looking at sales enablement tools. We're going from 15 reps to 40 by end of year, and our current approach isn't scaling.

**[0:32] Jordan:** That's a significant jump. Can you tell me more about what "not scaling" looks like day-to-day?

**[0:40] Priya:** Sure. Right now, our top performer, Derek, closes at about 35% — which is great. But our average is closer to 18%. The gap is too big. When I dig into why, it's almost always discovery. New reps don't ask the right questions, they don't uncover pain, and they end up in feature-dump demos that go nowhere.

**[1:10] Jordan:** That's a common pattern. Have you measured how long it takes new reps to get to quota?

**[1:18] Priya:** It's painful. About 6 months on average. And honestly, we've lost two reps in the past year who never got there — they just couldn't figure out our complex sale. We sell AI solutions to mid-market, so it's technical and relationship-driven at the same time.

**[1:42] Jordan:** That 6-month ramp is significant. Have you calculated what that costs the business?

**[1:50] Priya:** Not precisely, but I've done back-of-napkin math. If each rep should generate $800K annually, and they're at 50% productivity for 6 months, that's roughly $200K in lost pipeline per new hire. We're hiring 25 people this year, so...

**[2:15] Jordan:** Five million dollars in potential lost productivity.

**[2:20] Priya:** *pauses* When you say it like that... yes. That's the number that keeps me up at night.

**[2:28] Jordan:** Let me make sure I understand the full picture. You mentioned discovery is the main gap. Are there other skill areas where you see inconsistency?

**[2:38] Priya:** Objection handling is the other big one. We compete against larger players — Snowflake, Databricks — and reps struggle with the "why would I go with a smaller vendor" question. Our best people have crisp answers. Everyone else fumbles.

**[3:00] Jordan:** How do you handle coaching and training today?

**[3:05] Priya:** Honestly? It's ad-hoc. Derek does ride-alongs when he has time. Samantha, our CEO, will sometimes jump on calls and give feedback afterward. But there's no system. It's completely dependent on who has bandwidth.

**[3:28] Jordan:** And what's the executive team's appetite for investing in this? Is there budget allocated?

**[3:35] Priya:** Yes. Samantha and Marcus — our CTO — agreed we need to solve this before we scale. We've set aside $150 to $180K for sales enablement tooling. We looked at hiring more trainers, but the math doesn't work — we'd need 3-4 full-time coaches, and that's over $500K in headcount.

**[4:00] Jordan:** That makes sense. Technology tends to scale better than people for this problem. You mentioned Samantha and Marcus — who else would be involved in this decision?

**[4:12] Priya:** Jennifer, our CFO, will need to sign off on anything over $100K. And our IT team will need to review integrations since we use Salesforce as our CRM.

**[4:28] Jordan:** Got it. Let me ask about timing — is there a milestone or event driving urgency?

**[4:35] Priya:** Our Q2 sales kickoff is April 20th. Ideally, we'd have a solution in place and reps trained before then. If we miss that window, we're looking at Q3, and by then we'll have 25 new people who've already developed bad habits.

**[5:00] Jordan:** That's a firm deadline then — you need to be live by mid-April. Let me share what I'm thinking. Based on what you've described — discovery gaps, objection handling, 6-month ramp time, scaling from 15 to 40 — this sounds like a strong fit for our platform. We specialize in exactly this: AI-powered roleplay to practice discovery, objection handling, and competitive scenarios. Our customers typically see ramp time cut in half.

**[5:35] Priya:** Half? So 3 months instead of 6?

**[5:40] Jordan:** That's the average. Some do better. We had a data infrastructure company similar to DataFlow — they got to 2.5 months. I can share that case study. The key is repetition — reps can practice 10 scenarios a day, get instant feedback, and iterate. It's like a flight simulator for sales.

**[6:05] Priya:** That's compelling. We tried recording calls and doing reviews, but no one has time to watch hours of recordings.

**[6:15] Jordan:** Exactly. Our AI does the analysis, highlights coachable moments, and gives managers a summary. So instead of watching a 45-minute call, they see a 2-minute clip of the key moment with specific feedback already drafted.

**[6:35] Priya:** That would save our managers a ton of time. What about customization? Our sale is pretty unique — we need to qualify technical requirements early, and that's not a standard sales methodology.

**[6:52] Jordan:** Great question. We support custom methodologies. In fact, several of our customers have added their own qualification stages to MEDDIC. We could add a "Technical Validation" stage specifically for DataFlow. Let's talk about that in a follow-up with your team.

**[7:15] Priya:** I'd like that. What would the next steps look like?

**[7:20] Jordan:** I'd suggest a technical deep-dive with Marcus. We can walk through the Salesforce integration, data security — which I know is important for a data company — and the custom methodology options. Then a demo for you and the team. Sound good?

**[7:40] Priya:** Yes. Let me coordinate with Marcus's calendar. Can you send over some information I can share internally?

**[7:48] Jordan:** Absolutely. I'll send a one-pager, the case study I mentioned, and our security overview. Would it be helpful to include a sample ROI model based on the numbers you shared — the 6-month ramp, 25 new hires?

**[8:05] Priya:** That would be very helpful for Jennifer.

**[8:10] Jordan:** Perfect. I'll have that over by end of week. Thanks for the time, Priya. I'm excited about this opportunity.
