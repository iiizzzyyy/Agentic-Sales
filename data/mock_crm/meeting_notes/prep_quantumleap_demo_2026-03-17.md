## Custom Scenarios to Demo

### Scenario 1: "Why a 30-person startup over DataRobot?"
- AI prospect: "I've heard of DataRobot — they're the market leader. Why would I take a risk on QuantumLeap?"
- Good response elements: Focus on mid-market fit, implementation speed (6 weeks vs 6 months), pricing transparency, dedicated support
- Score dimensions: Confidence, specificity, avoiding feature dumps

### Scenario 2: "We're thinking of building in-house"
- AI prospect: "My engineering team says they can build this. Why would I buy?"
- Good response elements: TCO of build vs buy, opportunity cost, ongoing maintenance, time to value
- Score dimensions: Business acumen, handling technical objections, urgency creation

### Scenario 3: Technical Buyer Persona
- AI prospect: Skeptical VP of Engineering asking about data security, integration complexity, ML model accuracy
- Good response elements: Confidence on technical questions, knowing when to bring in SE, not over-promising
- Score dimensions: Technical credibility, qualification skills

### Scenario 4: Pricing Pushback
- AI prospect: "Can you do 20% off? We're a startup with limited budget."
- Good response elements: Hold the line (QuantumLeap doesn't discount), redirect to value, offer alternatives (fewer seats, quarterly)
- Score dimensions: Negotiation skills, value articulation, professional firmness

## Key Objections to Prepare For

**"How does this compare to Gong/Chorus?"**
- They analyze calls after they happen (reactive)
- We let reps practice before calls happen (proactive)
- Many customers use both — but if budget limited, practice ROI is higher (prevent problems vs analyze them)

**"What's the time commitment?"**
- 10-15 minutes per session, 3x per week = 30-45 min total
- Can practice on mobile between meetings
- ROI: 45 min/week vs 6-month ramp improvement

**"How do we measure success?"**
- Dashboard tracks: practice frequency, scenario scores over time, skill improvement trends
- Suggest 30-day pilot metrics: practice completion rate, score improvement, manager satisfaction
