# Discovery Call Methodology

## Overview
The discovery call is the most critical stage of the sales process. A well-executed discovery sets up every subsequent conversation. Poor discovery leads to stalled deals and lost opportunities.

## Call Structure (45-60 minutes)

### 1. Opening (5 min)
- Thank them for their time
- Confirm agenda and time available
- Brief company intro (30 seconds max)
- Transition: "I'd love to learn more about your situation before diving into our solution"

### 2. Current State (15 min)
Key questions:
- "Walk me through how your team currently handles [relevant process]"
- "What tools are you using today?"
- "How many people are involved?"
- "What does success look like for this process?"

### 3. Pain Discovery (15 min)
Key questions:
- "What's working well that you want to keep?"
- "What would you change if you could?"
- "When this doesn't work well, what's the impact?"
- "How does this affect you personally?"

### 4. Future State (10 min)
Key questions:
- "If you solved this, what would be different in 6 months?"
- "What have you tried before?"
- "What's driving the urgency to solve this now?"

### 5. Qualification (10 min)
MEDDIC framework:
- **Metrics**: "How would you measure success?"
- **Economic Buyer**: "Who ultimately approves this purchase?"
- **Decision Criteria**: "What factors will drive your decision?"
- **Decision Process**: "Walk me through how you've made similar decisions"
- **Identify Pain**: "On a scale of 1-10, how urgent is solving this?"
- **Champion**: "Who else is passionate about solving this?"

### 6. Next Steps (5 min)
- Summarize key findings
- Propose logical next step
- Confirm attendees and timing
- Send calendar invite before ending call

## Red Flags
- Prospect won't share budget range
- No clear timeline or event
- Can't identify economic buyer
- "Just looking" with no pain

## Green Flags
- Specific pain with business impact
- Active evaluation with timeline
- Multiple stakeholders engaged
- Willing to share internal context

## Post-Call Checklist
- [ ] Log notes in CRM within 1 hour
- [ ] Send follow-up email with summary
- [ ] Share relevant content/case studies
- [ ] Schedule next meeting
- [ ] Update deal stage and forecast
