# Sales Playbook - Sample

## Discovery Call Framework

### Objectives
- Understand the prospect's current situation and pain points
- Identify decision-makers and buying process
- Qualify the opportunity (BANT: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)
- Establish next steps

### Opening (2-3 minutes)
- Thank them for their time
- Confirm the meeting duration
- Set the agenda: "Today I'd like to learn more about your current challenges with [area], understand your goals, and see if there's a potential fit. Does that work for you?"

### Discovery Questions

**Situation Questions:**
- Can you walk me through your current process for [relevant area]?
- How long have you been doing it this way?
- What tools or solutions are you currently using?

**Problem Questions:**
- What challenges are you facing with your current approach?
- How is this impacting your team's productivity?
- What happens when [specific pain point] occurs?

**Impact Questions:**
- How much time/money does this cost you per month?
- What opportunities are you missing because of this?
- How does this affect other parts of the business?

**Need-Payoff Questions:**
- If you could solve this, what would that mean for your team?
- What would success look like in 6 months?
- How would this impact your [revenue/efficiency/team morale]?

### Common Objections & Responses

**"We're already using [Competitor]"**
Response: "That's great - it shows you've already recognized the importance of solving this problem. Many of our customers switched from [Competitor] because [specific differentiator]. What made you choose them originally, and how well is it working for you now?"

**"We don't have budget right now"**
Response: "I understand budget timing is important. Let me ask - if budget weren't a constraint, would this be something you'd want to move forward with? [If yes] What's your planning cycle for next year's budget? We could explore options that fit your timeline."

**"I need to talk to my team/boss"**
Response: "Absolutely - this should be a team decision. Who else would need to be involved in evaluating this? I'd be happy to schedule a follow-up call where we can include them and address any questions they might have."

**"The price is too high"**
Response: "I appreciate you sharing that. Let me make sure I understand what you're comparing it to. When you say the price is too high, is that compared to [competitor], doing nothing, or a budget number you had in mind?"

### Closing the Discovery Call
- Summarize key pain points you heard
- Confirm fit and interest
- Propose specific next steps with dates
- Send calendar invite before hanging up

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Target Companies
- Size: 50-500 employees
- Industry: B2B SaaS, Professional Services, Technology
- Revenue: $5M - $100M ARR
- Location: North America, Europe

### Target Personas
- **Primary:** VP of Sales, Head of Revenue Operations
- **Secondary:** CRO, Sales Director, Sales Enablement Manager
- **Influencer:** Sales Reps, Account Executives

### Buying Signals
- Recently raised funding
- Hiring for sales roles
- New sales leadership
- Using outdated tools
- Growing team rapidly
- Mentioned competitors on social media

## Competitive Battle Cards

### vs. Competitor A
**Their Strengths:**
- Strong brand recognition
- Lower price point
- Established integrations

**Their Weaknesses:**
- Limited customization
- Poor customer support
- Outdated UI

**Our Differentiators:**
- Modern, intuitive interface
- Dedicated customer success
- Flexible pricing
- AI-powered features

**When to Use:**
- Prospect values support and partnership
- Needs customization
- Has complex workflows
